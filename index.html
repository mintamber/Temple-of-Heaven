天坛祈年殿

The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests

蓝瓦承天，白石托殿。祈年殿以圆形殿身、三重屋顶和宏大的木构，把古代中国的敬天礼制凝成一座建筑。

An imperial hall where architecture becomes cosmology: blue tiles for Heaven, white marble for ritual elevation, and timber structure for sacred order.

圆形殿宇，指向苍穹 Its circular plan and triple roof turn the building into an image of the sky.

一座国家礼制建筑A monument of imperial ritual order

01
1420

始建于明永乐时期

天坛与北京城营建相伴而生，是明清皇帝祭天、祈谷的重要场所。

Built with imperial Beijing, it became a supreme site for worshipping Heaven and praying for harvests.

祈谷

为天下祈丰年

在农业文明中，丰收关乎国运。皇帝在此以“天子”身份代天下祈年。

Harvest ritual linked the emperor, the people, agriculture, and the Mandate of Heaven.

1998

世界文化遗产

天坛以建筑、景观与宇宙观的统一，成为中国文化遗产的代表。

A UNESCO World Heritage landmark of architecture, landscape, and cosmology.

外观：蓝瓦、红墙、白石Exterior: blue roof, red wall, white marble

02

圆，象征天；
高，产生敬畏。

祈年殿没有复杂的叙事，却有极强的视觉秩序：圆形、层叠、对称、上升。人站在台基下，自然感到尺度的转换。

The hall speaks through form: circular, layered, symmetrical, and ascending. Its scale turns space into reverence.

04

它不是一座孤立的殿宇，而是一种世界观。

祈年殿把天、地、君王、农时与建筑秩序联系在一起。它的壮丽，来自形式之美，也来自礼制之重。

The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests is not merely a building. It is a worldview made visible through architecture.
